Caleb A. Aldridge, Nature Conservationist

I am a research assistant and PhD student studying decision making in fisheries resources under the guidance of Drs. Michael Colvin and Leandro (Steve) Miranda. We’re housed in the Wildlife, Fisheries and Aquaculture Department within the College of Forest Resources at Mississippi State University. I’m supported by the Mississippi Cooperative Fish and Wildlife Research Unit and the Mississippi Department of Wildlife, Fisheries, and Parks.

My studies and research focus on sampling and monitoring protocols of state fisheries agencies across the U.S. and the decision-making process used to take action using metrics from such protocols. Part of my research is building a web application to be used by MDWFP fisheries biologists in constructing lake management plans based on monitoring data and will integrate decision analysis when considering alternative actions. To learn more about my research, or past projects, visit my research page or view my curriculum vitae.
